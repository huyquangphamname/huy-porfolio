The Challenge: Hidden Risks in Growth

Top-line growth masked underlying inefficiencies. A significant portion of transaction volume (the "Mass Market") was generating minimal net profit, and revenue was dangerously concentrated in just two key markets.

My Role: Strategic Diagnosis

  • Performed Pareto Analysis to isolate the "Vital Few" brands vs. the "Useful Many".
  • Quantified the "Phantom Revenue" effect (Zero-Revenue orders).
  • Mapped the Market Dominance Matrix to visualize platform dependency risks.

Strategic Implications

The analysis revealed that a "one-size-fits-all" strategy was failing. Malaysia required a distinct video-first approach, while the "Unidentified Brand" segment needed a cost-efficiency overhaul.

Technical Findings Summary

1. Data Integrity: Imputed "Unidentified Brand" for ~10% of records to preserve 8.5% of Gross Sales volume, ensuring accurate Long Tail analysis.

2. Zero-Revenue Orders: Thousands of valid orders yielded $0, confirming aggressive sampling. Recommended review of sampling ROI.

3. Market Matrix: "The Big Three" (PH-Shopee, TH-Shopee, TH-Lazada) act as the engine room. Systemic risk is concentrated here.
